The Debts Recovery Appellate Tribunal (DRAT) Mumbai Recruitment 2026 has been announced for multiple Group B and Group C posts on deputation basis! Published on 14-05-2026 vide No. DRAT/MUM/vacancy/Deputation/2026-27, the Ministry of Finance, Department of Financial Services is inviting applications from eligible officers/officials of Central Government/State Government/Courts/Tribunals for 70 vacancies across 6 posts at DRAT Mumbai and Debts Recovery Tribunals (DRTs) under its jurisdiction. The maximum age limit for appointment on deputation shall not exceed 56 years as on the closing date of receipt of applications. Find all details about DRAT Mumbai Deputation Recruitment 2026, including eligibility, vacancy details, and how to apply here.

A total of **70** posts are available for this recruitment.

## How to Apply

1. Download DRAT Mumbai Deputation Recruitment 2026 Official Notification PDF
2. The period of deputation including the period of deputation in another ex-cadre post held immediately preceding this appointment in the same or some other Organisation or Department of the Central Government shall ordinarily not exceed 3 years.
3. Officials who volunteer for the post shall not be allowed to withdraw their names later on.
4. The parent department will ensure that the official is clear from Vigilance point of view and no disciplinary case is either pending or contemplated against them as given in the proforma at Annexure-II before forwarding the application.
5. Post applied for and place of post applied for
6. Name, Designation and Office Address (in Block Letters)
7. Date of Birth
8. Date of entry into Government Service
9. Date of Retirement
10. Educational and other qualifications (SSC/Xth, HSC/XIIth, Degree, Postgraduate, Any other — with Year of Passing and Percentage/Grade)
11. Service particulars clearly indicating experience, educational qualification, and place of posting for which applied — in the prescribed proforma at Annexure-I & II
12. Attested photocopies of APARs for the last 5 years up to 31.03.2026
13. Certificate from Parent Department/Office that the official is clear from Vigilance point of view and no disciplinary case is pending or contemplated (Annexure-II)
14. Applications must be routed through Proper Channel only; directly submitted applications will not be entertained.
15. The Debts Recovery Appellate Tribunal, Mumbai and Debts Recovery Tribunals are quasi-judicial bodies set up under the provisions of the Recovery of Debts and Bankruptcy Act, 1993, as amended from time to time.
