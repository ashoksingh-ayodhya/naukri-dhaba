The Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In), functioning under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), has invited online applications for 133 Scientist-'B' posts (equivalent to Group-'A') on a direct recruitment basis. Shortlisting for the Personal Interaction/Interview stage will be based on a valid GATE score of 2024, 2025, or 2026. Eligible candidates can apply online from 14 July 2026 to 17 August 2026 (5:30 PM).

Level 10 of the 7th Central Pay Commission (7th CPC) has released an official notification for CERT-In Scientist-B Recruitment 2026: 133 Vacancies, GATE-Based Selection, Apply Online. 
A total of **133** posts are available for this recruitment.
Candidates must have 17.08.2026 as minimum educational qualification.

## How to Apply

1. Visit the official CERT-In website: https://www.cert-in.org.in/
2. Step 1 - Registration: Register with a valid email ID and active mobile number
3. Step 2 - Fill Online Application Form: Complete the application and upload all required documents
4. Step 3 - Download Confirmation Page: Submit the form finally and download the Confirmation Page - the application is treated as complete only after this step
5. Applications not conforming to the advertisement requirements are liable to be rejected
6. Qualifications through Open Universities/Distance Education must be approved by the Distance Education Council and, where necessary, AICTE, as per Gazette Notification No. 44 dated 1 March 1995
7. Candidates working with Central Govt./State Govt./PSU/Autonomous bodies must furnish an NOC confirming no pending disciplinary/vigilance case
8. No TA/DA will be paid for attending document verification or interview
9. Interviews will be conducted at Delhi or another location/mode approved by the competent authority; no request for postponement or change of location will be entertained
10. Candidates must validate their GATE score on the GATE Results portal on the day of interview/document verification using their own GATE login credentials
11. Court of jurisdiction for any dispute is Delhi
