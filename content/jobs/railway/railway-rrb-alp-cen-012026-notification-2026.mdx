The Railway Recruitment Boards (RRBs) have officially announced the Assistant Loco Pilot (ALP) Recruitment 2026 with 11,127 openings. The detailed notification was published on 14 May 2026. Candidates who meet the age criteria of 18 to 30 years and hold Matriculation + ITI or Diploma/Degree in Engineering can now submit their online applications from 15 May 2026 to 14 June 2026. Direct links to download the notification and apply online are shared below.

Department has released an official notification for RRB ALP (CEN 01/2026) Recruitment 2026 Notification Out - Apply Online for 11,127 Assistant Loco Pilot Posts. 
A total of **11127** posts are available for this recruitment.

## How to Apply

1. Age proof - Matriculation / SSLC / 10th class certificate
2. Educational qualifications (Matriculation onwards) - marksheets and certificates
3. ITI / Diploma / Degree certificate in prescribed trade / discipline
4. SC / ST / OBC-NCL / EWS / Ex-Servicemen certificate (if applicable), in prescribed format and issued by competent authority
5. Self Screening Medical Declaration Report (Annexure VIII A & VIII B) - to be submitted on CBAT day
6. NOC from present employer (if applicable, for Government servants)
7. Only one application per candidate is accepted - multiple applications to one or more RRBs will result in rejection of all applications and permanent debarment from RRB/RRC exams.
8. Candidates awaiting results of their final prescribed qualification examination are NOT eligible to apply.
9. Candidates who have undergone Lasik or any other corrective eye surgery are not eligible for the post of ALP.
10. Selection of RRB, once submitted, is final and cannot be changed at any later date.
11. Details filled in the "Create an Account" form (including mobile number, email ID) and chosen RRB cannot be modified at any stage after account creation.
12. Examination fee is refundable (after deducting bank charges) only to candidates who appear in CBT-1; rejected or absent candidates are not eligible for refund.
13. Refund will be credited only to the Aadhaar-seeded bank account; candidates must ensure their Aadhaar is linked to an active bank account.
14. Mobile phones, electronic gadgets, watches, earphones, Bluetooth devices, calculators, books, pen, food items, and all other listed banned items are strictly prohibited inside the test centre.
15. Examination centre, date, and shift allotted in the e-call letter are final - no requests for change will be entertained under any circumstances.
